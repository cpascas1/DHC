FH vs. FN

Financial Hardship vs Financial Navigation

Financial Hardship is a situation in which a person cannot keep up with debt payments and bills or if the amount that is needed to pay each month is more than the amount a person earns, due to a circumstance beyond their control while Financial Navigation is designed to help patients in high-cost drug treatments by providing financial solutions to assist with their out of pocket costs.

Financial Hardship Status in EPIC

  • FR3 - no longer qualified for FH since patient has been approved previously
  • FH Approved - approved for FH
  • FH Pending - No balance will be sent to collections

Financial Navigation Status in EPIC

  • FN Enrolled - Patient is currently enrolled in a Co-Pay Program or Foundation Assistance for a high-cost drug treatment.
  • FN Review - Patient is on a high-cost drug treatment but may not qualify for a program at this time. Assistance is based on the Drug CPT code and Dx. Foundation assistance is based on eligible funding and diagnoses. Sometimes a patient has an eligible diagnosis but there is no funding at the time of the appointment.

Things to Remember for Financial Navigation:

  • Co-Pay Program: Patients with commercial insurance who are enrolled in a Drug Manufacturer's Assistance Program.
  • Foundation Assistance: Patients with Government insurance (Medicare/Medicare Advantage/Medicaid and Tricare) who are enrolled in a Foundation providing financial assistance.
  • PFC Billed Program Activity: Patients enrolled in a copay program or foundation. Financial Navigation Team has contested the patient's transaction and billed the appropriate program for reimbursement. Qualified Patients are eligible for a one-time discount up to 100% on their current balance with Duly Health and Care.

Note: When patient is calling regarding questions or concern for their copay assistance/program we can connect them to Financial Navigation Team : 630-432-6350

Financial Hardship Screening

Prior to offering a patient financial hardship please verify the patient’s income does not exceed federal poverty guidelines. The guidelines below are for the standard hardship. Oncology/BCC approvals are based on the same FPL but have a sliding % of approval. Patient’s income cannot exceed 400% of the Federal Poverty Guidelines.

Documents required for approval:

  • Signed application
  • Proof of Income
    • Last 3 Paychecks
    • Unemployement Benefit Summary Letter
    • Disability or Social Security Letter
    • Last 3 Bank Statements
  • Proof of Dependencies
    • Most Recent Tax Return Documents (Top 2 pages required)
  • Letter/Explanation for Request

Failure to produce the required documents by the date on the application will be considered incomplete, and balance may be subject to collections review.

Exclusions:
Patients are not eligible for hardship if charges are considered Cosmetic, Durable Medical Equipment, or part of a third-party claim (Workers Compensation/Auto-Accident). Additionally, any charges that have been sent to a third-party agency for collections are not eligible for Financial Hardship. Patients who have been previously approved and do not meet the criteria for BCC or Oncology Hardship programs are not eligible to reapply. DuPage Medical Group Financial Hardship is a once in a lifetime offer.
