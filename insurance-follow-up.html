Duly Insurance Follow-up Related Call

Process Steps

  1. Obtain all necessary information from the patient to determine the concern. (e.g., Date of service, current procedural codes, diagnosis codes, total amount etc.)
  2. Review the patient's account and provide any information that can assist with the concern of the call.
    Note: Below are some points to remember in reviewing the account:
    • Check prior Customer Relations Management (CRM) tickers for additional information pertaining to charges in question.
    • Check account notes for additional information pertaining to charges in question.
  3. Determine the type of insurance follow up call for further information and details.

If...

State of Illinois plan
Note: Patient will call and inquire about a patient due amount but there is still an amount pending with the plan.

Then...

Advise the patient of the following information regarding the State of Illinois Insurance Plans:

  • The State of Illinois Insurance Plan is generally weeks behind in making payments to providers.
  • Insurance processes a claim and sends an Explanation of Benefit (EOB) to the patient; the providers are notified via on-line EOB.
  • The actual insurance payment may not be received by the provider until many months later.
  • We can bill the patient for the copay, deductible, coinsurance or other liability before we receive the contracted payment from the plan.
  • Patients will be billed their portion and be expected to pay, while the contractual adjustment and insurance payment due will remain in the "Insurance Due" column.
  • The contractual adjustment is not applied until the payment is received by the plan.

Create a CRM ticket using the pool CRM Optum AR - Insurance Follow Up. (Refer to Duly Create CRM in Epic)
Note: User should make sure first that there is no existing CRM ticket created for the same concern.

If...

One-Time Negotiations, sending of required documents
Note:

  • Insurance companies may request "One-Time" Negotiations for services.
  • The Insurance Follow-Up team receives and handles these requests.

Determine the number of days the documents were faxed to Insurance Follow-up department.

Not more than 10 business days

More than 10 business days
Note: It has been more than 10 business days and there is still no update from the department

Then...

Advise the caller to fax their request to the attention of the Insurance Follow-Up Department at fax # 630-545-6010 (all insurance plans).
Inform the caller to please allow 10 business days for processing of their faxed request.

Inform the caller to call back after a full 10 business days.

Contact center representative should forward an email to the Patient Account Representative (PAR) Supervisor to advise about the call.
Be sure to include all the detail/contact information in the email as well as in the CRM.
Do not forward the account to the PAR Pool, the Contact Center Representative can complete the account after forwarding the email to the PAR Supervisor.

If...

Patient requests a Letter of Medical Necessity for their own appeal to their health plan

Then...

Patient should contact the physician's nurse line to request a Letter of Medical Necessity.
If the patient is going to appeal with their plan and not pay any open balances-customer service should suggest a payment plan.

If...

Timely Filing concerns

Contracted insurance is the caller requesting to file the claim after timely filing.

Obtain the following information:

  • Name of insurance representative
  • Phone number of insurance representative
  • Call reference number

Create a CRM ticket to the insurance pool.
(Refer to Duly Create CRM in Epic)
Note: Include the reason why the insurance representative is requesting the claim be filed.
