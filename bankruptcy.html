Duly Bankruptcy Related Call

Overview

The process below provides additional information and details whenever contact center receives a bankruptcy related call.

Bankruptcy helps people who can no longer pay their debts get a fresh start by liquidating assets to pay their debts or by creating a repayment plan.

Additional information about bankruptcy paperwork:
  • The meeting of the creditors document shows the file date; and bankruptcy adjustments would be applied on dates up to and including the file date. Any other dates are left on the account in self pay or collections.
    • Meeting of creditors is a meeting at which the bankruptcy trustee and the creditors get to ask questions under oath about the patient's bankruptcy petition and the documents that are required to provide the trustee.
  • The collections department may also review a letter from an attorney as long as it shows the Debtor's name, SS#, Case #, Chapter #, and file date.
  • Discharge papers are only used to remove and close a bankruptcy; but the meeting of the creditors is what is reviewed for adjustments.
  • A Chapter 7 usually closes within a few months; a Chapter 13 can stay open for up to 5 years.
    • Chapter 7 - This chapter- of the Bankruptcy Code provides for "liquidation" - the sale of a debtor's nonexempt property and the distribution of the proceeds to creditors.
    • Chapter 13 - patient will continue making payments on those balance throughout the court-instructed repayment plan; afterwards, the unsecured debts may be discharged

Process Steps

Patient/caller would like to submit bankruptcy paperwork

Advise caller/patient that they can send the paperwork through mail, fax or email.

Once received a CRM ticket will then be created and account will be on FR2 status.

Bankruptcy CRM ticket shows Topic is CRM Collections and Subtopic is Bankruptcy Paperwork Rcvd.
